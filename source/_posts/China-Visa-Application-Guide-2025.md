## Introduction

Planning a trip to China? The first step is getting your visa. This comprehensive guide will walk you through the entire process, from determining which visa you need to submitting your application.

## Types of China Visas for Tourists

### L Visa (Tourist Visa)
The most common visa for travelers visiting China for tourism purposes.

**Single Entry**: Valid for 3 months, allows one entry
**Double Entry**: Valid for 3-6 months, allows two entries  
**Multiple Entry**: Valid for 6-12 months, unlimited entries

## Required Documents

![Passport and Documents](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1544636331-e26879cd4d9b?q=80&w=1000&auto=format&fit=crop)

1. **Valid Passport**
   - Must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your planned stay
   - At least two blank visa pages

2. **Visa Application Form (Form V.2013)**
   - Complete online at the Chinese Visa Application Service Center website
   - Print and sign

3. **Recent Passport Photo**
   - 33mm x 48mm
   - White background
   - Taken within last 6 months

4. **Proof of Travel**
   - Round-trip flight booking
   - Hotel reservations for entire stay
   - OR invitation letter from Chinese host

5. **Visa Fee**
   - Varies by nationality and processing time
   - Typically $140-200 USD for US citizens

## Application Process

### Step 1: Complete Online Application
Visit [visaforchina.cn](https://www.visaforchina.cn) and fill out the form completely.

### Step 2: Book an Appointment
Schedule your appointment at the nearest Chinese Visa Application Service Center (CVASC).

![Airport Travel](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1436491865332-7a61a109cc05?q=80&w=1000&auto=format&fit=crop)

### Step 3: Submit Documents
Bring all required documents to your appointment. You may need to provide biometric data (fingerprints).

### Step 4: Collect Your Visa
Processing typically takes 4-5 business days. Express service (2-3 days) available for additional fee.

## Processing Times & Fees

| Service Type | Processing Time | Fee (US Citizens) |
|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Regular | 4-5 business days | $140 |
| Express | 2-3 business days | $170 |
| Rush | 1 business day | $200 |

## Important Tips

- **Apply early**: Start your application at least 1 month before travel
- **Check validity**: Your visa is valid from the issue date, not your entry date
- **Keep copies**: Always carry copies of your visa and passport
- **Register**: Some cities require hotel registration within 24 hours of arrival

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I extend my visa while in China?**
A: Yes, but it requires visiting the local Public Security Bureau (PSB) and providing valid reasons.

**Q: Do I need a visa for a 24-hour transit?**
A: No, China offers 24-hour visa-free transit for most nationalities.

**Q: Can I work on a tourist visa?**
A: No. Working on an L visa is illegal and can result in deportation.

## Conclusion

Getting a Chinese visa is straightforward if you prepare your documents carefully. Start early, double-check everything, and you'll be exploring the Great Wall before you know it!
